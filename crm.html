      Cold Call Script
      5-phase cheat sheet · objection handling · booking close
      GOAL: Book the discovery call
      Script
      01Opener
      02Surface Pain
      03The Hook
      Pushback
      04Objections
      Finish
      05Book the Call
      Phase 01 — first 30 seconds
      The Opener
      You are not pitching anything yet. You are earning 2 minutes.
      🎯
      The only goal of this call is to book a discovery call.
      You don't know their bottleneck yet. Don't pretend you do. Don't sell agents. Don't explain AI. Just get 30 minutes on the calendar.
      Opening line
      "Hey [Name], this is [Your Name] from Work Floor. We help HVAC businesses implement AI into their operations — not generic software, custom-built systems specific to how your business runs. Quick question before I say anything else: what's the one thing in your business right now that's either costing you the most time or the most money?"
      Stop talking. Let them answer. That answer is your entire pitch.
      If they ask "what do you actually do?"
      "We get on a discovery call with HVAC business owners, figure out exactly where they're losing time or money — whether that's leads, dispatch, follow-up, reporting, whatever — and then we custom-build autonomous AI agents that handle those problems. The system grows with the business. As AI improves, we upgrade it. You're not buying software. You're getting a partner."
      If they push back immediately — "not interested"
      "Totally fair. One question before I let you go — are you running at full capacity right now, or is there somewhere in the business you feel like you're leaving money on the table?"
      Most owners will hesitate. That hesitation is your opening.
      Phase 02 — find the crack
      Surface the Pain
      You're not diagnosing yet — that's the discovery call. You just need them to feel the problem.
      Q1
      "When a lead comes in after hours — what actually happens to it?"
      → Most say "honestly, not much." That's your hook.
      Q2
      "How much time does your team spend on stuff that feels repetitive — scheduling, follow-ups, reporting?"
      → Listen for frustration. Don't jump in. Let it build.
      Q3
      "When you look at your numbers at the end of the month — do you feel like you have a clear picture of where revenue is coming from and where it's leaking?"
      → Most owners don't. Visibility is a universal pain.
      Q4
      "What does your business look like in two years if nothing changes?"
      → This creates forward pressure. They start selling themselves.
      Q5
      "If you could fix one thing that's either costing you money or eating your time — what would it be?"
      → This is the bridge to the hook. Whatever they say, your next line is Phase 3.
      Phase 03 — one line pivot
      The Hook
      After they name their pain, this is your only pitch. Keep it short.
      Universal hook — works for any bottleneck they name
      "That's exactly what we dig into on a discovery call. We don't come in with a pre-built solution — we spend 30 minutes understanding your business specifically, then we build autonomous agents custom to how you operate. Whether it's lead capture, dispatch, reporting, follow-up — the agent retrieves the data, surfaces the insight, and handles the action. You get the ROI without adding headcount.

      I'm not asking you to commit to anything. I just want 30 minutes to see if there's a fit."
      What autonomous agents actually do — if they ask
      "Think of it as an employee that never sleeps. It retrieves information from your systems — job data, customer history, lead activity — brings it together, and takes action based on rules we build together. Books the follow-up. Flags the cancellation. Pulls the weekly revenue report. It's not a chatbot. It's a system that does real operational work inside your business."
      Why us vs. just buying software
      "Software is generic. It's built for everyone, which means it's optimized for no one. What we build is specific to your workflow, your CRM, your team. And as AI keeps improving — and it will — we upgrade your system. You don't have to chase the technology. That's our job."
      The partnership framing — use this if they ask about pricing
      "We're not a vendor, we're a partner. We don't hand you a product and disappear. We stay in it with you. As your business grows, the agents scale with it. As AI evolves, we implement the better systems. The relationship is built around your ROI, not a subscription renewal."
      Phase 04 — pushback
      Objection Handling
      Click an objection to see the response.
      "We already have software for that."
      "Good — we don't replace it, we sit on top of it. Your CRM, your scheduling tool, your job management platform — we connect to all of it. The agents pull from those systems and take action across them. You keep the tools you know. We add the intelligence layer those tools don't have."
      "We're not really into AI / AI doesn't work for us."
      "That's fair — a lot of AI tools are gimmicks. What we build isn't a chatbot or a dashboard feature. It's an autonomous system that does real operational work: retrieves your data, identifies what needs to happen, and acts on it. Your team never has to touch it. You just see the output — filled jobs, recovered leads, clean reports.

      I'm not asking you to trust AI. I'm asking for 30 minutes to show you what it actually does in an HVAC business."
      "What does it cost?"
      "That's exactly why we do a discovery call first — the scope determines the price. We build custom, so we don't quote until we understand your business. What I can tell you is that everything we build is tied to a return. If the math doesn't make sense for your operation, we'll tell you on the call. We're not interested in a bad fit."
      "I need to think about it / talk to my partner."
      "Completely understand. The discovery call is actually the right place to bring your partner — it's not a sales pitch, it's a working session to map your operation. Nothing to decide before that.

      Can we get 30 minutes on the calendar for both of you? I'll send a calendar link right after this call."
      "Bad time — we're in the middle of busy season."
      "Honestly, busy season is exactly when the problems are loudest — missed leads, dispatch chaos, techs waiting on information. That's the data we'd use to build the right system.

      I'm not asking for time now. Can we schedule 30 minutes two weeks out when things settle? The conversation will be more valuable with real pain fresh in your mind."
      "How do I know this actually works?"
      "You shouldn't take my word for it — that's exactly why we do the discovery call before anything else. We map your specific operation, show you what an agent would do in your workflow, and you can decide if the logic makes sense before we build a single thing.

      You're not buying anything on the call. You're just seeing if it's real."
      "Why do you focus on HVAC?"
      "HVAC businesses have a specific set of operational patterns — seasonal demand, dispatch complexity, after-hours leads, recurring maintenance — that are highly automatable. We know the workflows, we know the CRMs, and we can move fast because we're not starting from scratch every time.

      It also means we're not a generalist agency pitching you something we've never done before."
      Phase 05 — the only ask
      Book the Discovery Call
      This is the entire goal. One ask, one close.
      The ask — say this exactly
      "I'd like to get 30 minutes on the calendar with you — no pitch, no commitment. We map your operation, identify the biggest place you're losing time or money, and I'll show you what an autonomous agent would actually do about it. If it doesn't make sense for your business, I'll tell you. What does your schedule look like this week or next?"
      If they say yes
      "Perfect. I'll send you a calendar link right now — takes 10 seconds to book. What email should I send it to?"
      Send it while they're on the phone. Don't wait.
      If they stall
      "Tell you what — I'll send the link now and you can pick a time when it's convenient. No follow-up pressure. A link in their inbox is better than no commitment."
      Lower the friction. A link in their inbox is better than no commitment.
      What the discovery call actually is — set this expectation
      "On the call we'll spend 30 minutes on three things: where your business is losing time, where it's losing money, and whether an autonomous agent is the right answer for those specific problems. You'll leave with clarity on your operation even if we never work together."
