# SELLING ANGLES & PITCH GUIDE — Fieldline AI
**Internal Sales Reference | Updated 2026-03-19**

---

## Universal Frame — Lead With the Relationship

> "You focus on running your business. We handle everything AI."

Don't open with features. Open with the relationship. Specific outcomes are proof points, not the pitch.

**Universal Pitch:**
> "We get on a call, learn exactly where your business is leaking money or time, build the AI system that fixes it, and give you a dashboard to watch the results. We manage everything. You never touch it."

---

## The Discovery Call — Run This First

This is the product. The call is where you find the exact angle that will close them.

**5 Questions:**
1. "When a customer texts or calls after hours — what happens to that lead?"
2. "How often does a job cancel in a given week, and what do you do with that slot?"
3. "Walk me through how a tech quotes a job on-site — how long does that take?"
4. "What does your customer list look like — when did you last reach out to old customers?"
5. "If you could fix one thing that's costing you money right now, what would it be?"

Their answers tell you which agent to lead with. You're not selling a package — you're solving their #1 problem first.

## Pitching by Bottleneck

### They're losing after-hours leads
> "We put a Concierge on your business. Every lead that texts or calls after hours gets a response in 60 seconds. Every missed call gets a follow-up within 5 minutes. You never touch it. Guarantee: 5 recovered leads in 30 days or second month free."

**The hook:** Most HVAC contractors miss 30-40% of inbound contacts. At $300-800 per job, that's real money walking out every week.

---

### They're quoting too slowly
> "We build a Closer. Your tech marks what they found in Jobber. A proposal shows up on their phone in under 60 seconds with the right price and margin. They present it on-site while the customer is still standing there ready to say yes."

**The hook:** Speed to proposal is the #1 driver of close rate. A customer who gets a quote 2 days later has already called three competitors.

---

### Their schedule has gaps from cancellations
> "We build a Dispatcher. When a job cancels, we automatically text your waitlist and fill the slot before you even know it opened. Every cancellation becomes a filled appointment."

**The hook:** The average HVAC contractor loses 4-6 appointment slots per month to cancellations. At $300 average, that's $1,200-1,800 in avoidable lost revenue every month.

---

### Their old customer list is dead
> "We build a Strategist. It scans everyone who hasn't called you in 12 months, identifies who's due for a tune-up, and organizes campaigns to fill your slow weeks with high-margin maintenance calls. Your dead list becomes your best lead source."

**The hook:** A customer who used you before is 5x more likely to book again than a cold lead. Most HVAC contractors never follow up with past customers.

## HVAC Contractor Profiles

### Jobber Contractors — Primary Target
**Who:** Owner-operated, 2-8 techs, $500K–$2M revenue, feel the pain personally
**Sell to:** Yes — this is the MVP customer
**CRM advantage:** Full Jobber API from day one

**Selling Angle:**
> "Jobber handles your invoices and scheduling. We handle everything Jobber can't — the 2 AM emergency call, the canceled job slot, the proposal your tech couldn't generate on-site."

**The gap Jobber leaves:**
- No after-hours response
- No automatic cancellation fill
- No proposal generation from diagnostics
- No customer reactivation from old list
- No urgency triage

---

### ServiceTitan Contractors — High Value, Handle Carefully
**Who:** Larger operations, already paying $1,500-3,000/month for ST, financially sophisticated
**Sell to:** Yes — but acknowledge their investment, don't compete with it
**API status:** Partner API approval pending (4-8 weeks) — sell the vision, confirm availability before promising ST-native features

**Selling Angle:**
> "ServiceTitan is your system of record. We're your system of action. ST tells you what happened — we make things happen before you open your laptop."

**The gap ServiceTitan leaves:**
- Rigid workflow structure, no flexible custom automation
- No autonomous after-hours response
- No real-time proposal generation from tech diagnostics
- No customer list mining or block special campaigns
- Steep learning curve — owner still managing too much

**Why ST contractors pay more:** They already believe in paying for tools. They're not price-sensitive. They're buying the intelligence layer ST doesn't have.

---

### No CRM Contractors — Build Them One
**Who:** Running on spreadsheets or nothing — usually $1M+ revenue making this painful
**Sell to:** Yes, but higher scope, higher price
**Approach:** "We'll build you a proper CRM and layer the agents on top. You go from zero to fully automated in one engagement."
**Price:** Higher setup fee, higher retainer. Custom agreement.

## Objection Handling

**"Our CRM already does this."**
> "Your CRM captures the data. We do everything that happens next — qualify the urgency, respond in 60 seconds, fill the cancellation, generate the proposal, chase the invoice. Your CRM is the record. We're the action layer."

**"We can't afford another subscription."**
> "This isn't a subscription — it's a retained partner. The math is simple: we need to recover 10 leads or fill 5 canceled slots per month to pay for itself. That's the guarantee. Everything beyond that is margin."

**"How much does it cost?"**
> "Depends on what we find on the call. Starts at $1,500/month. Let me understand your situation first — I'd rather quote you exactly what you need than give you something generic."

**"I'm already looking at [competitor tool]."**
> "Good — use it. We're not a CRM, we're not replacing what you have. We're the autonomous layer on top of it. Most of our clients use multiple tools — we connect them."

**"I need to think about it."**
> "Fair. One thing worth knowing — we only work with one contractor per market area. If you want time to think, let me know so I can hold your spot while you decide."

**"Can I try it free first?"**
> "No free trial — the setup fee is the commitment signal that makes the partnership work. But the guarantee is real: 5 recovered leads in 30 days or your second month is free. The risk is on us."

**"I use ServiceTitan."**
> "Good — we're building the intelligence layer ST doesn't have. I'll be honest with you about what's available today vs. what's coming in the next 60 days. Worth a call to see if the timing works."

## The Guarantee — How to Say It

**On the call:**
> "We guarantee 5 recovered leads in your first 30 days or your second month is free. A recovered lead is one that came in through the system, got a response in 60 seconds, and resulted in a booked or followed-up appointment. It's logged automatically — no arguing, no fine print. The data is always there."

**Why this works:** It removes the primary risk objection before they raise it. The setup fee handles the commitment signal. The guarantee handles "what if it doesn't work."

---

## What Works (Keep These)

- Lead with the discovered pain, not the solution
- Give specific numbers ($300-800 per job, 4-6 cancellations/month)
- State the guarantee early — removes objections before they're raised
- Make the CTA low-commitment ("discovery call" not "sign up")
- Reference their specific CRM — generic outreach gets ignored
- Sell the relationship before the features

## What Doesn't Work (Avoid)

❌ "AI-powered" or "revolutionary" — too generic
❌ Feature lists — nobody reads them
❌ Fixed tier menus — sounds like everyone gets the same thing
❌ Technical language (n8n, webhook, Supabase, API)
❌ Free trials — we don't offer them
❌ Long pitches — if it needs scrolling, it's too long
