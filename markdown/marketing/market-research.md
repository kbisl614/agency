# HVAC Market Research Analysis
**Source:** Perplexity Voice-of-Customer Research | March 2026  
**Purpose:** Product feature prioritization + landing page copy direction

---

## What the Data Confirms — Keep These

### Cancellation Fill (Workflow 6) — Keep, prioritize even harder
The "standby list / backfill open time" language came up organically in contractor forums. They already think in this mental model. You're not teaching them a new concept, you're automating something they already know they need.

### After-Hours Lead Capture (Workflows 1 + 2) — Keep, this is your hero feature
The 47-hour average response time vs. your 30-second response is the sharpest contrast in the entire pitch. *"Leads that arrive after 7pm disappear by 8am"* is a direct contractor quote. This should be the first thing on the page. Or something extremely similar to this should be on the first page.

### Daily Owner Summary (Workflow 12) — Keep
"Saved 40 hours a month" is how satisfied contractors describe good tools. The summary email is the proof-of-value delivery mechanism every morning. Don't cut it.

### Audit Trail / ROI Logging (Workflow 3) — Keep, it's your retention engine
They stick with tools that show clear time and revenue savings. The audit trail is what makes them renew month 2, 3, 4.

---

## What the Data Suggests You Should Add

### 1. Missed Call Recovery via SMS — Add This Explicitly
The research surfaced *"27% of all calls go unanswered, 13 missed calls/month, $3,900 in lost revenue."* Right now the system captures web/SMS leads. But contractors are bleeding on **phone calls they miss during the day** — not just after hours. Adding a "missed call → instant SMS follow-up" workflow is trivial with SignalWire and maps directly to a number contractors already know.

> **Example trigger:** If they miss a call during the day, the system texts the caller within 60 seconds:  
> *"Hey, we just missed your call — we're on a job. What's going on with your system? We'll get back to you within the hour."*

**Revenue impact:** $3,900/month by their own documented math.

### 2. Speed-to-Lead Tracker on the Dashboard — Add This
The Hatch data shows *"88% of contractors take more than 5 minutes to reply, most take a full day."* If the dashboard shows contractors their average response time **before** (e.g., 6 hours) vs. **after** (30 seconds), that's a retention and upsell weapon. It makes the ROI visual and undeniable.

### 3. HCP Assist Differentiation — Address in Positioning, Maybe Product
HouseCall Pro already has an after-hours AI call handler. It's script-based and rigid. The product needs at least one visibly smarter feature — **cancellation fill via SMS outreach** is the clearest one. When selling to HCP users, lead with cancellation fill, not lead capture, because they'll say *"we already have that."*

---

## What the Data Suggests You Should Deprioritize

### Tech ETA Monitor (Workflow 11) — Push to Month 3+
No contractor mentioned ETA anxiety as a top pain in the research. It's a nice-to-have. Don't build it before you have paying clients.

### Google Review Monitor (Workflow 15) — Cut from the Pitch Entirely for Now
Zero mentions of review management as a pain point. Contractors care about leads, cancellations, and dispatch. Reviews didn't come up once. It's a feature that sounds impressive in a deck but doesn't close deals.

### Seasonal Outreach (Workflow 13) — Keep Building, Don't Lead With It
It's real value but it's a month 2 conversation. Contractors need to see immediate ROI in the first 30 days. Lead capture and cancellation fill deliver that. Seasonal campaigns are the upsell story.

## Voice-of-Customer Phrases to Mirror in Copy

Pull these exact phrases into headlines, subheads, and body copy. This is how contractors talk — not how the product is described.

**On missed leads:**
- *"Leads that arrive after 7pm disappear by 8am."*
- *"If I don't reply immediately, they've already moved on to someone else."*
- *"Every call matters… never miss a call and never miss an opportunity."*
- *"In emergency situations, nobody wants to wait for hours."*

**On dispatch and scheduling:**
- *"Constantly evolving schedule with moving parts that need to be adjusted on the fly."*
- *"Invites appointment mistakes and route inefficiencies."*
- *"Backfill open time when a job falls off the schedule."*
- *"Scrambles will be less chaotic when communication is automated."*

**On outcomes they trust:**
- *"Saved us about 40 hours a month."*
- *"Turned scheduling from one of our biggest challenges into one of our greatest strengths."*
- *"Handles the client communication layer with minimal manual effort."*

## HCP Competitive Note

HouseCall Pro is actively marketing **HCP Assist** — an after-hours AI call handler that books jobs automatically. Their positioning: *"Turn after-hours inquiries into booked jobs."*

**Their weakness:** Template-driven, script-based, no cancellation fill, no SMS waitlist outreach, no dispatcher automation layer.

**Your angle with HCP users:** Don't compete on after-hours call answering. Lead with cancellation recovery and dispatcher automation — features HCP Assist doesn't touch.
