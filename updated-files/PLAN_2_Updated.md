**The Problem: The Slowness Tax**

78% of customers buy from the first responder. Yet the average service
business takes hours --- sometimes days --- to reply to emergency
requests and web inquiries. When an AC fails in summer, when a pipe
bursts at 2 AM, when a customer cancels and a slot goes idle --- the
business without an autonomous response system loses that revenue to a
competitor who was faster.

This Slowness Tax is universal across service businesses. It\'s not an
HVAC problem. It\'s a human-labor-as-bottleneck problem. And it\'s the
same problem we solve for every vertical we enter.

  will start hearing about AI agents from trade publications and peers.

  type of business.\'

**The Headless Operations Model**

We position every client business as having two separate, complementary
engines:

-   Their existing tool (CRM, calendar, booking software) is the
    Logistics Engine --- manages invoicing, scheduling, records

-   Our partnership is the Growth Engine --- captures leads 24/7, fills
    cancellations, manages customer lifecycle

-   Outcome: the owner stops being a dispatcher and becomes a CEO. The
    dashboard shows exactly how much revenue was generated while they
    slept.

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------
  **The Retained Partner Layer:** Beyond the technology, we are the
  client\'s ongoing AI advisor. When NemoClaw launches, we evaluate it,
  test it, and roll it out for them. When their CRM ships a new feature,
  we assess whether it changes their setup. The client pays once and gets
  a permanently evolving system --- not a static product that ages.
