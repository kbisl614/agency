# HVAC SaaS: Selling Angles by CRM Stack
**Last Updated:** March 2026 | Internal Sales Reference

---

## Overview

Use this document to tailor your sales pitch based on which CRM a contractor uses. Each section covers whether to sell, the right angle, pricing, and integration caveats.

---

## ServiceTitan Contractors

**Should you sell to them?** Yes — but carefully.

These are your highest-value prospects. Already paying $1,200+/month for software, larger operations, financially sophisticated enough to understand ROI conversations.

**Selling angle:**
> "ServiceTitan is your system of record. We're your system of action. ST tells you what happened — we make things happen before you even open your laptop."

**The hook:** ServiceTitan has some AI features but they're basic and locked inside their ecosystem. We add the autonomous response layer ST doesn't have — 2 AM lead capture, cancellation SMS recovery, proactive outreach.

**Pricing:** $2,000–$2,500/month. These contractors are bigger, have more technicians, more leads, more cancellations. The ROI math scales up with them. They won't blink at $2,500 if you show $8,000+ in recovered revenue.

**⚠ Caveat:** Can't fully integrate ST until you have partner API access. Interim play: sell the vision, onboard via Zapier/Make.com as a bridge, plug in the real API once approved. Apply for ST partner access now — approval takes 4–8 weeks.

---

## Jobber Contractors

**Should you sell to them?** Yes — this is your MVP customer.

Jobber contractors are smaller, scrappier, more open to trying new tools. More likely to be owner-operators who feel the pain of missed leads personally.

**Selling angle:**
> "Jobber handles your invoices and scheduling. We handle everything Jobber can't — the 2 AM emergency call, the cancelled job slot, the dispatcher tasks eating your Saturday."

**The hook:** Jobber's automations are template-based and reactive. Ours are intelligent and proactive — NLP urgency classification, cancellation detection and fill, lead response in under 30 seconds 24/7.

**Pricing:** $1,500/month. Jobber contractors are price-sensitive compared to ST users. $1,500 is the sweet spot — significant enough to take seriously, justifiable with the ROI math.

**Integration advantage:** Full API access from day one. Fastest path to a live demo with real data. This is your first-mover platform.

---

## HouseCall Pro Contractors

**Should you sell to them?** Selectively.

HCP contractors sit between Jobber and ST in size and sophistication. Popular with residential HVAC contractors who want something simpler than ServiceTitan.

**Selling angle:**
> "HouseCall Pro is great for running your day-to-day. But it's not watching your business at 2 AM, it's not texting customers when a job cancels, and it's not chasing down unpaid invoices automatically. We are."

**The hook:** HCP users chose it because they wanted simple. Position your product as the intelligence layer that makes their simple CRM punch above its weight — without switching platforms. No migration, no retraining, no disruption.

**Pricing:** $1,200–$1,500/month. HCP contractors tend to run slightly smaller operations. Start at $1,200 to reduce friction, upsell to $1,500 once you prove ROI in month one.

**⚠ Caveat:** HCP's API is less mature. Be upfront that some features (deep calendar sync, real-time tech location) may have a short delay in availability.

---

## No CRM / Spreadsheet Contractors

**Should you sell to them?** No — not yet.

Without a CRM there's no data layer to sit on top of, no webhook to listen to, no job history to pull from. You'd be building their entire operations stack, not augmenting it.

**The exception — Full Stack Bundle:** If a no-CRM contractor is high-revenue ($2M+) and clearly feels the pain, offer a CRM onboarding + our system bundle. Help them get set up on Jobber, layer your product on top immediately after. Charge $3,000–$4,000/month. High-touch but high-margin, and creates a sticky long-term customer.

---


Regardless of platform, the offer framing stays the same:

> "30-day free trial. We set everything up. If we don't capture at least 5 leads and fill 2 cancellations in 30 days, you pay nothing."

This removes all risk objections and makes the CRM type irrelevant to their decision to try it. The CRM only determines integration complexity on the backend — the contractor doesn't need to think about that.
