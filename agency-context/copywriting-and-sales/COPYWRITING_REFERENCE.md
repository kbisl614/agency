## What We Are (Say This First, Internally and Externally)

**Productized agency. Not SaaS.**

We are a retained AI operations partner for HVAC contractors. We get on a discovery call, learn exactly where their business is leaking money or time, build the AI that fixes it, and run everything indefinitely. They never touch infrastructure. They see outputs every morning.

> "You focus on running your business. We handle everything AI."

This is the frame for every piece of copy on every surface. If a line sounds like it's describing a product feature rather than what we do *to* their business, rewrite it.

## Voice & Tone Rules

### Always Use
- ✅ Partnership language: "your AI operations partner," "retained partner," "we run it"
- ✅ First-person plural active: "We build," "We run," "We map" — not "the system handles"
- ✅ Outcome-first: what changed in their business, then how
- ✅ "You never touch it" — repeat this across sections
- ✅ "Discovery call first" — signals custom engagement, not a product purchase
- ✅ Contractor vocabulary: "jobs," "techs," "dispatch," "waitlist," "slot," "on-site"
- ✅ Specific numbers: $3,900/month, 60 seconds, 47 hours, $8,600/month
- ✅ "Logged automatically in your dashboard" — proof language, use near any claim
- ✅ Custom framing: "built for your operation," "scoped to what we find on the call"
- ✅ CRM-agnostic language: "connects with your existing CRM, whatever it is"

### Never Use
- ❌ SaaS language: "subscription," "platform," "features," "configure," "plans," "tiers"
- ❌ "AI-powered" or "cutting-edge" — generic, ignored
- ❌ "Trial" language of any kind — no free trial exists
- ❌ Vendor framing: "we provide a solution," "our product offers"
- ❌ Tech infrastructure as selling points: n8n, Supabase, API, webhook, NemoClaw, OpenClaw
- ❌ Old brand name: "DispatchHVAC" — name is Fieldline AI
- ❌ Tier names: "Foundation," "Partner," "Full Partner" — no fixed tiers exist
- ❌ Guarantee language: "5 recovered leads," "second month free" — removed entirely
- ❌ Upcharge references: do not mention pricing changes, upgrades to pricing, or future cost increases in any direction
- ❌ "No pitch" — we are pitching them; don't use this phrase
- ❌ Specific agent names in customer-facing copy: don't say "Concierge," "Closer," "Dispatcher," "Strategist" on the website
- ❌ Specific feature callouts implying a packaged product: don't mention "lead capture," "missed call recovery," or "daily summaries" as named features
- ❌ Locking to specific CRMs: don't say "only works with Jobber/ServiceTitan/HouseCall Pro"

## Pricing Copy

**The model:** No fixed tiers. Custom-quoted per engagement after discovery call.

**How to describe pricing on the website:**

> Engagements start at $1,500/month depending on the scope of the engagement. We scope everything on the discovery call. Some contractors need one agent. Some need four. We'll tell you exactly what we'd build and what it would cost before any money changes hands. Setup fee starts at $500 and scales with scope.

**Never show a tier table on the website.** No fixed packages. No named tiers.

**Never mention pricing changes in either direction.** Don't say "no upcharges," don't say pricing increases as you scale — just don't go there.

**Pricing reference ranges (internal only — for proposals, never website copy):**

| What gets built | Approx monthly |
|---|---|
| One agent | $1,500 |
| Two agents | $2,000–2,500 |
| Three agents | $2,500–3,500 |
| Full build (all agents) | $3,500–4,500 |
| No CRM — full build | Custom, higher |

Setup fee: $500 (single agent) → $1,500–2,000 (full build) → custom for no-CRM engagements.

## Replacing the Guarantee — Contract/Terms Framing

**The guarantee ("5 recovered leads in 30 days or second month free") has been removed entirely.**

Why: It was inherited from an earlier SaaS framing. On a custom engagement, a universal guarantee creates unmanageable promises — what "recovered lead" means varies by contractor and by what was built. It also signals transactional thinking, not partner thinking.

**What replaces it — on the website:**

> We scope the engagement on the call. If what we'd build makes sense for your operation, we'll outline terms and move forward. No generic packages, no pressure — just a clear proposal and a decision.

**What replaces it — in objection handling on calls:**

> "We don't do a generic guarantee because every engagement is custom. What we do is scope exactly what we'd build, show you the projected ROI, and agree on terms before anything starts. You'll know exactly what you're getting before you pay a dollar."

**Remove guarantee language from:** website, cold emails, selling angles, objection handlers, onboarding docs, all customer-facing copy.

## Layout Notes for Developers

- **Padding:** All sections need moderate breathing room — minimum 96px top/bottom on desktop, 64px on mobile. The page currently feels cramped. Every section should feel like it has space to breathe before the next one starts.
- **Stats row:** Align all three stat cells to `flex-start` vertically — uneven subtext lengths currently break the row height.
- **Step 3 card:** Give it a subtle visual distinction from Steps 1 and 2 — slightly brighter border or amber accent — to signal it's the ongoing permanent state, not just the final setup step.
- **Form button:** Cap at 280px width on desktop, centered. Full-width on mobile only.
- **Guarantee section:** Remove entirely. Replace with "No contracts until we've proven the fit" block.
