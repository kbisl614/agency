## Email Sequence Tips

**Timing:**
- Email 1: Day 0 (initial discovery)
- Email 2: Day 3-4 (if no reply)
- Email 3: Day 7-8 (final touch)

**Personalization Levels:**
1. First name in greeting
2. Mention their CRM (Jobber/ServiceTitan/HCP)
3. Specific revenue numbers relevant to their business
4. Reference to their area/market if possible

**Response Handling:**
- Busy signal: "Not right now" → Save for 30 days, try again
- Objection: Address directly, don't be salesy
- Interest: Move to call immediately (15-min discovery)
- No response after 3 emails: Archive and try again in 60 days

**Key Conversion Elements (What Works):**
- Lead with the problem (lost emergency calls = lost revenue)
- Give specific numbers ($150 per call, 8-12 missed per month)
- Remove risk (free trial, guarantee, no card)
- Use social proof (other contractors' results)
- Include CTA (book trial link)
- Keep it short (3-4 paragraphs max)

## Tracking & Follow-Up

Track responses in a simple spreadsheet:
- Recipient name / company
- CRM type
- Email 1 sent date
- Response status (no reply, replied, booked call, not interested)
- Email 2 sent date (if applicable)
- Final outcome

Contractors who don't respond to 3 emails typically aren't ready. Revisit in 60 days—companies' needs change, budgets open up, pain points grow.

---

## Anti-Patterns (What NOT to Do)

❌ Don't be salesy ("revolutionary," "cutting-edge," "AI-powered")
❌ Don't make it too long (keep it 3-4 short paragraphs)
❌ Don't use "you need this" language (let them decide)
❌ Don't make promises you can't keep (be conservative)
❌ Don't follow up immediately (minimum 3 days)
❌ Don't use generic subject lines ("Check this out," "Question for you")
❌ Don't forget the CRM name (make it specific, not generic "dispatch" talk)
